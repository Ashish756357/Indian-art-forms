Journey Through Indian Art History

Indus Valley Seals (c. 3300–1300 BCE)

Engraved stone seals with animal motifs and undeciphered script.

Lion Capital of Ashoka (c. 322–185 BCE)

A sandstone sculpture of four lions standing back-to-back.

Buddha Statue from Sarnath (c. 320–550 CE)

A sandstone statue of Buddha in a meditative pose.

Nataraja (c. 850–1250 CE)

A bronze statue of Lord Shiva as the cosmic dancer.

Akbarnama (c. 1526–1858 CE)

Miniature paintings illustrating scenes from Emperor Akbar's life.

Raja Ravi Varma’s Paintings (c. 1600–1947 CE)

Oil paintings depicting Indian mythology with European techniques.

Husain’s Horses (1947–present)

A vibrant, abstract painting of horses in motion.
